How We Rank

Our Methodology

Pole 2 Pole rankings are built on one principle: evaluate what actually wins basketball games — not what looks good on a highlight tape or plays well to a crowd of shoe brand recruiters.

We scout players in competitive environments across the U.S. and internationally, including FIBA competitions, Adidas Next Gen, Grind Session events, European league play, and BAL Academy circuits. We look at film. We talk to coaches. We track consistency over time, not single-game performances.

Every player in our system is ranked on the same global scale. A prospect from Žalgiris Academy is evaluated by the same standards as one from Montverde. That's the only way a global ranking means anything.

01
Skill & Craft
We evaluate a player's ability to create shots for themselves and others, handle pressure, and execute in critical moments. Skill is what remains after athleticism fades.
Shot creationBall handlingPassingFinishingManipulation
02
Basketball IQ
Decision speed, defensive rotations, weak-side awareness, connective passing, and the ability to read and react rather than execute a script. IQ is the hardest thing to teach and the most predictive of long-term success.
Decision makingOff-ball readsWeak-side instinctsCommunication
03
Winning Habits
How a player behaves when the game is close, when they're down, when their shot isn't falling, and when their team needs something from them that isn't their primary role. Character and competitive disposition are non-negotiable criteria.
CompetitivenessDefensive effortPace regulationEmotional stability
04
Projection & Context
Every player is ranked based on where we believe they project — not only where they are today. We account for competition level, system fit, age relative to class, and physical trajectory. A player in a weaker league who dominates and shows elite indicators ranks appropriately.
Physical trajectoryCompetition levelAge in classSystem independence
